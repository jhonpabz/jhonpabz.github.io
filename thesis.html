Urdaneta Catholic Center Sales & Inventory Management System

Project Study

The study of Point of Sale and Inventory Management System of Urdaneta Catholic Center aims to highlight the business transactions and to manage the inventory and sales reports. The developed system aimed to enhance and replace the existing manual process of the business as well as helping the proprietors, staffs and so as the customers in general. Furthermore, the data process including the customer information to manage their credit, purchased products, record management and sorting of hard copies of records when it is needed for transactions report. Problems encountered from the current manual process of the business can be minimized through adaptation of the proposed system. This tends to develop an online POS and Inventory management that will function more efficiently.

Visit Project

The main objective of the proposed system is to create a Management Information System which is Sales and Inventory Management Information System for Urdaneta Catholic Center that shows some of the processes for the admin and cashier.

Specifically, it sought to answer the following objectives: To determine the current processes implemented by Urdaneta Catholic Center along the following areas: customer information, sales, inventory and report generation; to identify the problems encountered in the existing process of Urdaneta Catholic Center in terms of: customer information, sales, inventory and report generation; specify the functional and non-functional requirements of the proposed system and to identify the hardware requirements and software requirements of the proposed system.

Upon the development of the proposed system, the researchers use the Rapid Application Development Model (RAD) because of its uniqueness and convenience; since the researchers worked as a group, RAD is really one of the best methods to use because it implies collaboration and interaction between the researchers and the client to define or analyze the existing problem on the current system. The researchers worked together so that they can make prototypes and other visual means to describe the problem to be solved
